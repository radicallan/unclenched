How Hearthflow works (MVP)

Start simple: a resident journey you can embed on your own site. No accounts, no jargon – just enough intelligence to sort homes into the right path and give everyone a clear next step.

Step 1 · Home & situation

The resident enters their address and a few key details: tenure, heating type, benefits, main comfort and bill issues. Where available, Hearthflow pulls EPC data to identify the home archetype.

Step 2 · Route & eligibility

A rules engine built around ECO4, Social Housing Decarbonisation Fund, Warm Homes Local Grant and local offers sorts the home into a likely route: scheme-backed, landlord-funded or able-to-pay.

Step 3 · Roadmap & next steps

The resident sees a three-phase upgrade roadmap with indicative impact and a clear next step – booking an assessment, registering interest or viewing trusted installers. Your teams see structured demand, not random enquiries.

Under the hood: the Hearthflow platform

Hearthflow is designed as a small ecosystem you can grow into – with distinct modules for journeys, logic, insight and quality.

Hearthpath

The resident-facing journey. Clean, accessible flows you can embed on your own domain, tailored to your schemes and language.

Hearthstack

The measure and roadmap engine. Encodes archetypes, sensible measure stacks and cost / impact ranges into three-phase plans.

Hearthscope

The insight console for councils and landlords. See where interest is, what residents are asking for, and which homes are clustering around which routes.

Hearthmark

A future quality layer. Structured scores for homes, projects and installers so you can see where your portfolio is genuinely improving, not just spending.

Who Hearthflow is for

The first version focuses on organisations already responsible for homes and schemes – and who want to join the dots between residents, stock, funding and delivery.

Local authorities

Climate and housing teams who need a resident-friendly entry point for ECO4/GBIS, SHDF and Warm Homes programmes – and a clearer view of where demand sits in their stock.

Social landlords

Housing associations and ALMOs who want to blend stock-level investment planning with resident-level signals about comfort, affordability and willingness to have works done.

Delivery partners

Retrofit and installation partners who would rather receive well-qualified homes and informed residents than cold lists and confused phone calls.
